About Me

My 1-Year Vision

Over the next year, I’m building and shipping products that reflect how people actually experience finance, learning, and connection—not just how they look in theory.

Part 1 — Navigating Finance with ML
My journey starts with understanding money at the most personal level. PlainCents focuses on day-to-day financial behavior—spending, habits, and patterns. From there, RiskFecta steps into uncertainty, risk, and decision-making under volatility using modern portfolio theory and forecasting. The path ends with an AI-powered financial education platform , designed to help Canadian newcomers build confidence navigating an unfamiliar financial system. The goal is to create low-pressure pathways for students to meet in the real world—through shared curiosity, exploration, and spontaneity. This project reflects my interest in building technology that nudges people off their screens, not deeper into them.

And because I love challenges and strategic thinking, I also built a neural network-driven chess engine—purely to push myself and learn how machines reason in complex decision spaces. Check out VectorMate.

WatStreet - Uwaterloo's Quantitative Finance Design Team

ML Engineer - Volatitly Contagion Explorer January 2026 - Present

Contributing to a research-oriented quantitative finance project focused on modeling and predicting volatility contagion across U.S. equity markets. The goal is to understand how shocks propagate between assets over time by combining temporal graph neural networks with classical econometric benchmarks.

Key Contributions:

  • Designing and training Temporal Graph Neural Networks (T-GNNs) to model rolling correlation structures and volatility spillovers across S&P 500 assets.
  • Benchmarking Graph Attention Network predictions against GARCH and HAR-RV econometric baselines using RMSE and Diebold-Mariano tests.
  • Implementing rolling-window training and evaluation pipelines to avoid look-ahead bias and assess model stability under different market regimes.
  • Applying sparse covariance estimation techniques (Graphical Lasso) to analyze dynamic inter-asset dependency graphs during periods of elevated market stress.

Python PyTorch Temporal Graph Neural Networks Graph Attention Networks Time Series Modeling Volatility Modeling GARCH HAR-RV Graphical Lasso Statistical Testing RMSE Diebold–Mariano Test
